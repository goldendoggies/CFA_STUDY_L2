Triangular Arbitrage

Triangular Arbitrage

Arbitrage opportunity if:

  • Dealer Bid > Internbank Offer
  • Dealer Offer < Interbank Bid

If these two conditions hold, there will NOT be an arbitrage opportunity:

  • Dealer Bid < Internbank Offer
  • Dealer Offer > Interbank Bid
  1. Compare dealer bid/offer with interbank bid/offer
  2. observe bid and offer on two pairs, find implied cross rate
    • May need to invert the bid and offer to make things comparable
    • Then multiply bids and offers to find implied cross rate

Mark to market

Mark to market of Forward currency rates

  1. Enter forward into offsetting position
  2. Add or subtract forward points appropriately to extrage rate
  3. subtract forward rate at initiation from forward offsetting rate, then multiply by notional amount
  4. Take result from forward mark to market and discount back to initiation using appropriate currency's discount rate

Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)

Reflects the relationship between foreign exchange rates and inflation differentials

Law of one price - same good in different countries should be same price when priced by common currency

Inflation differentials across contries cause nominal foreign exchange rates to adjust to equate real purchasing power (CPI) in the long run

Absolute Purchasing Power Parity

  • Observed at the general price level - Not specific Good(s)
  • Assumption: Price indices are identical - Same goods and services and same weights
    • Either goods are different or weights are different
  • Highly Unlikely to hold

Relative PPP

  • costs are constant over time
  • Generally: changes in foreign currency rate = inflation differential

Ex-Ante PPP

  • Expected changes in spot foreign exchange rates are driven by the expected differences in inflation rates
International Fisher Effect

Combines the currency foreign currency rate differentials and inflation rate differentials

Affecting Long Run Forex

Dealing with the CURRNET ACCOUNT and the CAPITAL ACCOUNT in a country

Macroeconomic Balance Approach

  • estimate how much interest rates need to adjust to close the gap between
    • current account imbalance
    • and its long term sustainable current account imbalance
  • focus on 'real' flows

External sustainability Approach - deals with capital account

  • Must have some sustainable level of
    • net foreign assets/GDP
    • net foreign liability/GDP
  • focus on 'financial' flows

Reduced Form Econometric Model

  • Focus on trends of macro variables to estimate equilibrium path for currency
    • net foreign assets positions
    • terms of trade
    • relative productivity
Current and Capital Accounts Flows and Affects on Currency

Impact on flows from real flows (current account) and financial flows (capital account) on the currency exchange rate

Current Account

  • Sum of all recorded transactions in traded goods, services, income and net transfer payments
  • Persistent Current Account surplus = currency appreciates over time
  • Persistent Current Account deficit = currency depreciatation over time
  1. Flow Supply/Demand Channel
  2. Portfolio Balance Channel
  3. Debt Sustainability Channel

Capital Account

  • Investment/financing flows are dominant factor in determining currency exchange rate movements (in short to medium term)
  • These flows occur more rapidly and affect more currnecies more quickly.
